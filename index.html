What is Bāzì?

Bāzì (八字) literally means "Eight Characters." It's one of the oldest forms of Chinese metaphysics, developed over 2,000 years ago and refined during the Tang dynasty (618–907 AD).

The moment you were born — your year, month, day, and hour — is encoded into eight Chinese characters that reveal your nature, strengths, and the energies flowing through your life.

🏛

The Four Pillars (四柱 Sìzhù)

Your birth data maps onto four "pillars," each holding two characters — one Heavenly Stem and one Earthly Branch — giving you eight characters total.

年Year Pillar
月Month Pillar
日Day Pillar
时Hour Pillar

The Day Pillar is your core self. Year reflects ancestry and early life. Month governs career and young adulthood. Hour reveals your inner world and later years.

⚖️

Five Elements (五行 Wǔxíng)

Every character belongs to one of five elements. Balance matters — too much or too little of any element shapes your personality and path in life.

Yin & Yang (阴阳 Yīn Yáng)

Every element appears in both Yin (陰, receptive) and Yang (陽, active) forms. Your chart's balance shapes how you engage the world versus how you process things within.

🌌

Destiny vs. Free Will

Bāzì shows tendencies and potential, not fixed outcomes. Think of it as a weather forecast — knowing the forecast helps you prepare, but you still choose where to go.

The goal is self-understanding: to work with your natural energy rather than against it.
